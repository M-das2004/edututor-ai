SOURCE: Student Handbook

CHAPTER 1: TYPES OF EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITIES
================================================

Sports and Physical Activities
--------------------------------
Regular physical activity is essential for students. Benefits include:
- Improves concentration and memory retention
- Reduces stress and anxiety before exams
- Builds teamwork and leadership skills
- Improves overall health and energy levels

Available sports: Cricket, Football, Volleyball, Kabaddi, Athletics, Table Tennis, Chess

For students in rural areas with limited facilities:
- Running (early morning 30 minutes)
- Yoga and stretching exercises
- Traditional Indian games like Kho-Kho, Gilli-danda

Cultural Activities
-------------------
Cultural activities help students develop confidence and creativity:
1. Annual Day celebrations — drama, dance, skits
2. Debate competitions — improves speaking and thinking skills
3. Elocution (speech) — builds confidence
4. Folk dance and classical music — connects to cultural roots
5. Art and craft competitions

Academic Clubs
--------------
Join or start academic clubs at your school:
- Science Club: Experiments, model-making, science fair preparation
- Mathematics Club: Puzzles, Olympiad preparation, fun with numbers
- Literature Club: Writing stories, poems, book discussions
- Quiz Club: General knowledge, competitive quiz preparation
- Computer Club: Basic programming, typing skills

CHAPTER 2: TIME MANAGEMENT FOR STUDENTS
=========================================

Balancing Studies and Activities
----------------------------------
Many students think extracurriculars reduce study time. Research shows the opposite — structured activities improve study discipline.

Sample Daily Schedule (School Day):
- 5:30 AM: Wake up, exercise/yoga (30 mins)
- 6:00 AM: Morning self-study (1 hour)
- 7:30 AM: Get ready, breakfast
- 8:30 AM – 2:30 PM: School
- 3:00 PM: Rest/nap (30 mins)
- 3:30 PM: Extracurricular activity (1 hour)
- 5:00 PM: Homework and school assignments
- 7:00 PM: Dinner
- 8:00 PM: Evening self-study (1-1.5 hours)
- 9:30 PM: Sleep

Weekly Planning
---------------
At the start of each week:
1. List all tests/assignments due that week
2. Allocate specific study hours for each subject
3. Keep one day (usually Sunday) lighter for revision
4. Do not study more than 2 hours continuously — take 10-minute breaks

CHAPTER 3: SCHOLARSHIPS AND OPPORTUNITIES
==========================================

Government Scholarships for Rural Students
-------------------------------------------
Several scholarships are available specifically for rural and meritorious students:

1. National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in)
   - Pre-matric scholarships (Class 1-10)
   - Post-matric scholarships (Class 11 and above)
   - Available for SC/ST/OBC and minority students

2. INSPIRE Scholarship (DST)
   - For students scoring in top 1% in Class 10 board exams
   - For pursuing science at Class 11-12 level
   - Amount: ₹80,000 per year

3. Pragati and Saksham (AICTE)
   - For girl students in technical education
   - Amount: ₹50,000 per year

4. State Government Scholarships
   - Each state has its own scholarship schemes
   - Check your state government's education portal

How to Apply for Scholarships
-------------------------------
Required documents (usually):
- Marksheet of previous class
- Income certificate of parent/guardian
- Caste certificate (if applicable)
- Bank account details (student's own account preferred)
- Aadhaar card
- School bonafide certificate

Tips:
- Apply early — many scholarships have deadlines
- Keep photocopies of all documents
- Ask your school teacher for help with the application
- Do not pay anyone to apply — all government scholarships are free to apply
