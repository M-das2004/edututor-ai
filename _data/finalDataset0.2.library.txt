CHAPTER 1: USING THE LIBRARY EFFECTIVELY
==========================================

How to Search for Books
------------------------
When looking for a book in the library:
1. Use the library catalogue (usually a computer terminal at the entrance)
2. Search by Title, Author, or Subject keyword
3. Note the Call Number — this tells you where the book is on the shelf
4. Books are arranged by subject using the Dewey Decimal System

Dewey Decimal System (Simplified):
- 000-099: General works, Computer Science
- 100-199: Philosophy, Psychology
- 200-299: Religion
- 300-399: Social Sciences
- 400-499: Language, Linguistics
- 500-599: Natural Sciences, Mathematics
- 600-699: Technology, Applied Sciences
- 700-799: Arts, Recreation
- 800-899: Literature
- 900-999: History, Geography

Reference Section
-----------------
The Reference Section contains books that cannot be borrowed — they must be used inside the library. These include:
- Encyclopaedias (like Encyclopaedia Britannica)
- Dictionaries and Thesaurus
- Atlases and Maps
- Year Books and Almanacs
- Government Reports

CHAPTER 2: READING AND NOTE-TAKING STRATEGIES
===============================================

SQ3R Method for Reading Textbooks
-----------------------------------
SQ3R stands for: Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review

1. SURVEY: Skim the chapter — read headings, subheadings, first/last paragraphs
2. QUESTION: Turn each heading into a question (e.g., "What is photosynthesis?")
3. READ: Read carefully to answer your questions
4. RECITE: Close the book and recall what you read (say it aloud or write it)
5. REVIEW: Review your notes and re-read unclear parts

This method improves retention by 40% compared to simple reading.

Note-Taking Techniques
-----------------------
Cornell Method:
Divide your page into three sections:
- Right column (large): Main notes during class
- Left column (narrow): Key words and questions after class
- Bottom section: Summary in your own words

Mind Mapping:
- Write the main topic in the center
- Draw branches for major subtopics
- Add smaller branches for details
- Use colors and symbols to remember better

CHAPTER 3: DIGITAL LIBRARY RESOURCES
======================================

Online Databases Available for Students
-----------------------------------------
Free resources every student should know:
1. NPTEL (nptel.ac.in) — Free engineering and science video lectures by IIT professors
2. DIKSHA (diksha.gov.in) — Government platform with NCERT content for all classes
3. e-Pathshala — NCERT eBooks, audio books, and videos (free)
4. SWAYAM — Free online courses with certificates from Indian universities
5. National Digital Library (ndl.gov.in) — Over 6 crore books and papers (free)

How to Download Free eBooks Legally
-------------------------------------
- NCERT official website (ncert.nic.in): All NCERT books from Class 1-12 free
- Project Gutenberg (gutenberg.org): Over 60,000 classic books free
- OpenLibrary (openlibrary.org): Borrow digital books free with library card
- Google Books: Preview many books, some fully available free

Evaluating Online Sources
--------------------------
Not all websites are reliable. Check:
1. Author: Is the author qualified? Are credentials listed?
2. Date: When was it published/updated? Is it recent enough?
3. Source: Is it from .gov, .edu, or a reputed organization?
4. Purpose: Is it trying to inform or sell/persuade?
5. Accuracy: Can you verify the facts from other sources?

CHAPTER 4: AVOIDING PLAGIARISM
================================

What is Plagiarism?
-------------------
Plagiarism is using someone else's words, ideas, or work without giving them credit. It is considered academic dishonesty and can lead to serious consequences.

Types of plagiarism:
1. Copy-paste plagiarism: Directly copying text without citation
2. Paraphrasing plagiarism: Changing a few words but keeping the same idea without credit
3. Self-plagiarism: Submitting your own previous work as new work

How to Cite Sources (Simple Format)
-------------------------------------
For a book:
Author Last Name, First Name. Title of Book. Publisher, Year.
Example: Sharma, Ramesh. Class 10 Science Textbook. NCERT, 2023.

For a website:
Author (if known). "Page Title." Website Name, Date. URL.
Example: "Photosynthesis." NCERT Portal, 2023. www.ncert.nic.in

When in doubt — cite the source. It is always better to over-cite than to plagiarize.
