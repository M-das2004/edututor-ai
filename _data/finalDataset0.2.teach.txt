UNIT 1: ELECTRICITY (PHYSICS)
==============================

Electric Current
-----------------
Electric current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor. It is measured in Amperes (A). 
Current (I) = Charge (Q) / Time (t)
Formula: I = Q/t

Think of it like water flowing through a pipe — the amount of water flowing per second is like electric current.

Ohm's Law
---------
Ohm's Law states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points, provided the temperature remains constant.

Formula: V = I × R
Where:
V = Voltage (in Volts)
I = Current (in Amperes)  
R = Resistance (in Ohms, Ω)

Example: A bulb has resistance 100Ω. If voltage is 220V, current = 220/100 = 2.2 A

Resistance
----------
Resistance is the property of a conductor that opposes the flow of electric current. 
Factors affecting resistance:
1. Length of conductor — longer wire = more resistance
2. Cross-sectional area — thicker wire = less resistance
3. Material of conductor — copper has less resistance than iron
4. Temperature — higher temperature = more resistance (for metals)

Series and Parallel Circuits
-----------------------------
Series Circuit:
- Components connected end to end
- Same current flows through all components
- Total resistance = R1 + R2 + R3
- If one component fails, entire circuit breaks

Parallel Circuit:
- Components connected across common terminals
- Voltage across each component is same
- 1/Total R = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3
- If one component fails, others keep working (used in home wiring)

UNIT 2: ACIDS, BASES AND SALTS (CHEMISTRY)
===========================================

Acids
-----
Acids are substances that produce H+ (hydrogen ions) in water solution.
Properties of acids:
- Sour taste (like lemon juice, tamarind)
- Turn blue litmus paper RED
- pH less than 7
- React with metals to produce hydrogen gas

Common acids: Hydrochloric acid (HCl), Sulfuric acid (H2SO4), Nitric acid (HNO3)
Natural acids: Citric acid (lemon), Acetic acid (vinegar), Lactic acid (curd)

Bases
-----
Bases are substances that produce OH- (hydroxide ions) in water solution.
Properties of bases:
- Bitter taste, soapy feel
- Turn red litmus paper BLUE
- pH greater than 7
- Neutralize acids

Common bases: Sodium hydroxide (NaOH), Calcium hydroxide Ca(OH)2
Natural bases: Baking soda, washing soda

pH Scale
--------
pH scale measures how acidic or basic a solution is. Range: 0 to 14
- pH 0-6: Acidic (lower = more acidic)
- pH 7: Neutral (pure water)
- pH 8-14: Basic/Alkaline

Neutralization Reaction
-----------------------
When an acid reacts with a base, they neutralize each other to form salt and water.
Acid + Base → Salt + Water
Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O

UNIT 3: LIFE PROCESSES (BIOLOGY)
==================================

Nutrition in Plants — Photosynthesis
--------------------------------------
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food using sunlight.

6CO2 + 6H2O → (sunlight + chlorophyll) → C6H12O6 + 6O2

In simple words: Carbon dioxide + Water → (using sunlight) → Glucose + Oxygen

Conditions needed for photosynthesis:
1. Sunlight
2. Chlorophyll (green pigment in leaves)
3. Carbon dioxide (from air through stomata)
4. Water (from soil through roots)

Human Digestive System
-----------------------
Digestion is the process of breaking down food into simpler substances that can be absorbed.

Path of food:
Mouth → Oesophagus → Stomach → Small Intestine → Large Intestine → Anus

Important organs:
- Mouth: Mechanical digestion (chewing) + saliva breaks starch
- Stomach: Gastric juice (HCl + pepsin) digests proteins
- Small Intestine: Main site of digestion and absorption; receives bile from liver
- Large Intestine: Absorbs water; undigested food exits as waste

Respiration
-----------
Respiration is the process of releasing energy from food.

Aerobic Respiration (with oxygen):
C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O + Energy (ATP)
Occurs in: mitochondria

Anaerobic Respiration (without oxygen):
In yeast: Glucose → Ethanol + CO2 + Energy
In muscles: Glucose → Lactic acid + Energy (causes muscle cramp)
